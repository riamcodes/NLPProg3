Word: overtime

Sense 1 (work done in addition to regular working hours) – 3 new sentences:
1) The union negotiated better overtime rates for night shift workers.
2) Employees must obtain supervisor approval before working overtime on weekends.
3) The new policy caps weekly overtime at 10 hours to prevent burnout.

Sense 2 (playing time beyond regulation, to break a tie) – 3 new sentences:
1) The championship game went into double overtime before a winner was decided.
2) They scored the winning goal just 30 seconds into the first overtime period.
3) After a scoreless overtime, the match was decided by penalty kicks.
